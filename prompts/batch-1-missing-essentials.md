# OpenEvidence Prompt — Essential Rehabilitation Exercises (Batch 1)

I'm building my clinical knowledge of commonly prescribed rehabilitation exercises across several regions. For each exercise below, please provide:

1. **Target muscles and EMG activation levels** (% MVIC where studied)
2. **Evidence-based dosing** — sets, reps, frequency, hold times from RCTs or systematic reviews
3. **Key coaching cues** supported by biomechanical research
4. **Regressions** (easier variations) and **progressions** (harder variations)
5. **Clinical indications** — which patient populations benefit most
6. **Contraindications or precautions**

Please cite specific studies (author, year, journal) for each exercise.

## Cervical Spine Exercises

### 1. Isometric Neck Strengthening (4 directions)
Applying manual resistance or using a hand to resist cervical flexion, extension, lateral flexion (bilateral), and rotation (bilateral) without allowing movement.
- What EMG activation levels are seen in the deep cervical flexors, SCM, scalenes, and cervical extensors during isometric holds?
- What resistance levels and hold durations are recommended for chronic neck pain?
- How does isometric training compare to dynamic training for neck pain outcomes?
- Evidence from Jull, Falla, or O'Leary studies on isometric cervical exercise?

### 2. Upper Trapezius Stretch
Seated or standing, laterally flex the cervical spine away from the target side while gently depressing the ipsilateral shoulder.
- Optimal hold duration and frequency?
- Does stretching the upper trapezius actually reduce EMG activity or muscle stiffness?
- Evidence for effectiveness in tension-type headache or cervicogenic headache?
- How does this compare to or complement active cervical ROM exercises?

### 3. Levator Scapulae Stretch
Similar to upper trap stretch but with cervical rotation toward the target side to bias the levator scapulae over the upper trapezius.
- What differentiates the stretch position for levator scapulae vs upper trapezius?
- Evidence for reducing neck pain or improving cervical ROM?
- Recommended dosing from clinical studies?

## Wrist & Hand Exercises

### 4. Eccentric Wrist Extension (Tyler Twist / Flexbar)
Using a Thera-Band FlexBar, perform a wrist extension twist eccentrically for lateral epicondylalgia (tennis elbow).
- What does the Tyler et al. (2010) RCT show for outcomes?
- EMG activation of ECRB, ECRL, and ED during the eccentric phase?
- Dosing protocol from the original study and subsequent validation?
- How does this compare to standard eccentric wrist extension with dumbbells?

### 5. Wrist Flexion/Extension Curls (Dumbbell)
Seated forearm-on-thigh wrist curls in both flexion and extension directions with light dumbbells.
- EMG comparison of wrist flexor curls vs extensor curls?
- Evidence-based dosing for wrist strengthening in general populations and clinical populations (post-fracture, tendinopathy)?
- What grip width and forearm position optimizes activation?

### 6. Finger Extension with Rubber Band
Place a rubber band around all five fingertips and spread the fingers apart against resistance.
- What muscles are activated (ED, EDM, intrinsics)?
- Evidence for use in lateral epicondylalgia, trigger finger, or general hand strengthening?
- Dosing recommendations from hand therapy literature?

### 7. Tendon Gliding Exercises (Finger)
Systematic progression through hand positions: straight → hook fist → full fist → tabletop → straight fist, to maximize differential tendon excursion.
- What is the evidence for tendon gliding after flexor tendon repair or carpal tunnel release?
- Which positions produce maximum FDS vs FDP excursion?
- Recommended frequency and repetitions from surgical rehab protocols?

### 8. Putty Pinch Variations (Tip, Lateral, Three-Jaw Chuck)
Using therapy putty, perform different pinch patterns to strengthen intrinsic hand muscles.
- EMG activation of thenar muscles, FPL, adductor pollicis during different pinch types?
- Evidence for effectiveness in thumb CMC OA, post-surgical hand rehab?
- How do force demands differ between tip pinch, lateral pinch, and three-jaw chuck?

## Lumbar Spine Exercises

### 9. Side Plank
Sidelying forearm plank for lateral core stability. Can be performed from knees or feet.
- EMG activation of quadratus lumborum, obliques, gluteus medius, erector spinae?
- What does McGill's research show about the side plank compared to other core exercises?
- Evidence for use in chronic low back pain and scoliosis?
- Dosing: hold duration, repetitions, progression criteria?

### 10. McGill Curl-Up
Modified curl-up with hands under lumbar spine, one knee bent, one straight. Minimal spinal flexion range.
- How does this differ biomechanically from a standard crunch or sit-up?
- What is the spinal compression load compared to sit-ups?
- EMG activation of rectus abdominis, obliques during McGill curl-up?
- Why does McGill recommend this over traditional crunches for LBP patients?

### 11. Pallof Press (Anti-Rotation)
Standing perpendicular to a cable or band anchor, pressing arms straight forward against rotational pull.
- EMG activation of obliques, TrA, rectus abdominis, erector spinae?
- Evidence for anti-rotation training in low back pain populations?
- Dosing: resistance, hold duration, sets/reps?
- Progressions from the literature (kneeling, overhead, with step)?

### 12. Hip Hinge / Deadlift Pattern
Standing hip hinge with bodyweight or light load. Focuses on posterior chain loading while maintaining neutral spine.
- EMG activation of erector spinae, gluteus maximus, hamstrings during hip hinge?
- What does the evidence say about deadlift training for chronic low back pain?
- How does a trap bar deadlift compare to conventional for spinal loading?
- Dosing for rehabilitation vs strength training contexts?
