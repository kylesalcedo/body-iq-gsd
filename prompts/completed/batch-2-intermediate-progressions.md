# OpenEvidence Prompt — Intermediate Progression Exercises (Batch 2)

I'm studying intermediate-level exercises that bridge early rehabilitation to functional fitness. For each exercise below, please provide:

1. **Target muscles and EMG activation levels** (% MVIC where studied)
2. **Evidence-based dosing** — sets, reps, frequency from RCTs or systematic reviews
3. **Key coaching cues** supported by biomechanical research
4. **Regressions** (easier variations) and **progressions** (harder variations)
5. **Clinical indications** — which patient populations benefit most
6. **Biomechanical considerations** — joint forces, common compensation patterns

Please cite specific studies (author, year, journal) for each exercise.

---

### 1. Split Squat / Bulgarian Split Squat
Staggered stance squat with rear foot on ground (split squat) or elevated on bench (Bulgarian). Unilateral lower extremity exercise.
- EMG comparison of quadriceps, gluteus maximus, and gluteus medius versus bilateral squat?
- Does rear foot elevation change activation patterns?
- Patellofemoral and tibiofemoral joint forces compared to bilateral squat?
- Evidence for use in ACL rehabilitation, patellofemoral pain, or hip strengthening?
- Recommended dosing and loading parameters?

### 2. Romanian Deadlift (RDL) / Single-Leg RDL
Hip hinge with barbell or dumbbells, knees slightly bent, hinging from hip. Single-leg version for unilateral training.
- EMG activation of hamstrings (BF, ST, SM individually), gluteus maximus, and erector spinae?
- How does RDL compare to Nordic hamstring exercise for hamstring activation?
- Single-leg RDL: what are the balance and gluteus medius demands?
- Evidence for hamstring injury prevention with RDL training?
- Dosing for rehabilitation vs athletic performance?

### 3. Copenhagen Adduction Exercise
Side-lying with the top leg supported on a bench, performing hip adduction by lifting the body with the bottom leg.
- Peak adductor longus activation compared to other adduction exercises?
- How does support position (knee vs ankle) affect loading and activation?
- Evidence for groin injury prevention in soccer and team sports?
- Dosing protocols from prevention and rehabilitation studies?
- Regression options for patients who cannot perform the full version?

### 4. Nordic Hamstring Exercise
Kneeling with ankles anchored, eccentrically lowering the body forward using hamstring control.
- Peak hamstring activation and which heads (BF long, BF short, ST, SM) are preferentially recruited?
- Evidence for hamstring injury prevention (meta-analyses)?
- Dosing from the original Mjolsnes (2004) and subsequent prevention programs?
- What are the reported injury rates during the exercise itself?
- Progressive regression/progression strategies?

### 5. Turkish Get-Up
Multi-step exercise transitioning from supine to standing while holding a weight overhead.
- Which muscles are activated at each phase (roll, bridge, sweep, lunge, stand)?
- EMG data for shoulder stabilizers, core, and hip muscles?
- Evidence for shoulder stability, core function, or injury prevention?
- Recommended loading and programming?
- Is this exercise studied in rehabilitation populations?

### 6. Farmer's Carry (Loaded Carry)
Walking while holding heavy weights at sides (bilateral) or one side (unilateral suitcase carry).
- EMG activation of forearm flexors, upper trapezius, core musculature during loaded carries?
- What does research show for grip strength development?
- Trunk stabilization demands: bilateral vs unilateral carry?
- Evidence for functional capacity improvement in clinical populations?
- Dosing: load relative to body weight, distance, duration?

### 7. Front Plank and Variations
Prone forearm plank. Variations include plank with arm reach, leg lift, body saw, and long-lever plank.
- EMG activation of rectus abdominis, obliques, TrA, erector spinae during standard plank?
- How do variations change activation patterns (arm reach, leg lift, body saw)?
- What is the recommended hold duration — is there a ceiling effect?
- Evidence for plank training in low back pain?
- How does plank compare to dynamic core exercises for strength vs endurance outcomes?

### 8. Side Plank with Hip Abduction (Copenhagen Plank)
Side plank position with top leg performing hip abduction, or bottom leg performing adduction off a bench.
- EMG of gluteus medius, obliques, and quadratus lumborum?
- How does adding hip abduction change core activation compared to standard side plank?
- Evidence for use in groin pain, hip OA, or lateral trunk weakness?
- Dosing from clinical studies?

### 9. Step-Up Variations (Forward, Lateral, Cross-Over)
Stepping onto a box or step from different directions.
- EMG comparison of quadriceps, gluteus maximus, and gluteus medius across step-up directions?
- How does step height affect activation levels?
- Patellofemoral joint forces during step-ups vs squats?
- Evidence for use in knee OA, patellofemoral pain, and post-ACL rehab?
- Dosing and progression of step height?

### 10. Kettlebell Swing
Explosive hip hinge with two-hand kettlebell swing to chest height.
- EMG activation of gluteus maximus, hamstrings, erector spinae, and core?
- How does the swing compare to deadlift for posterior chain activation?
- Evidence for power development, low back pain, or cardiovascular fitness?
- Is this exercise safe and effective in clinical populations?
- Dosing: weight selection, sets/reps, work-to-rest ratios?

Please cite specific studies with author, year, and journal for each exercise.
