# OpenEvidence Prompt — Advanced / Sport-Return Exercises (Batch 3)

I'm studying advanced exercises used in late-stage rehabilitation and return-to-sport programs. For each exercise below, please provide:

1. **Target muscles and EMG/force data** where studied
2. **Evidence-based dosing** — sets, reps, frequency, intensity from RCTs or sport science literature
3. **Key coaching cues** supported by biomechanical research
4. **Prerequisites** — what baseline strength/ROM is needed before attempting
5. **Injury risk considerations** — joint forces, common errors, contraindications
6. **Return-to-sport criteria** that include this exercise

Please cite specific studies (author, year, journal) for each exercise.

---

### 1. Box Jump (Bilateral)
Jumping from the floor onto a box/platform. Used for lower extremity power development and landing mechanics training.
- Peak ground reaction forces during takeoff and landing?
- EMG activation of quadriceps, gluteals, and calf during jump vs landing phase?
- What box heights are used in rehabilitation vs performance contexts?
- Evidence for use in ACL return-to-sport protocols (Grindem, Myer, Hewett studies)?
- Landing mechanics cues to reduce ACL injury risk?

### 2. Depth Jump / Drop Jump
Stepping off a box, landing, and immediately jumping vertically. Plyometric exercise for reactive strength.
- What are the ground reaction forces compared to box jumps and countermovement jumps?
- Optimal drop height for training vs injury risk threshold?
- EMG and force differences between depth jump and countermovement jump?
- Evidence for improving rate of force development in athletes?
- What strength prerequisites (e.g., squat 1.5x body weight) are recommended before depth jumps?

### 3. Lateral Bounds (Single-Leg)
Single-leg lateral hops for frontal plane power and dynamic stability.
- EMG activation of gluteus medius, peroneus longus, and ankle stabilizers?
- Ground reaction forces and knee valgus demands during lateral bounding?
- Evidence for use in ACL prevention programs (Hewett, Myer, Noyes studies)?
- How do lateral bounds compare to single-leg hop for distance as a return-to-sport criterion?
- Dosing in plyometric programs?

### 4. Power Clean / Hang Clean
Olympic lift derivative: explosive triple extension (ankle, knee, hip) pulling a barbell from floor or hang position.
- EMG activation of quadriceps, hamstrings, gluteals, erector spinae, and upper trapezius?
- Peak power output compared to squat, deadlift, and kettlebell swing?
- Is the power clean studied in rehabilitation contexts, or only athletic performance?
- What technique prerequisites exist before loading?
- Evidence for improving athletic performance (sprint speed, jump height)?

### 5. Overhead Squat
Full squat with arms extended overhead holding a barbell or dowel.
- What shoulder, thoracic, hip, and ankle mobility requirements exist?
- EMG activation of shoulder stabilizers, core, and lower extremity during overhead squat?
- Is this used as an assessment tool (FMS) or primarily as a training exercise?
- Evidence for identifying movement compensations?
- Common compensatory patterns and what they indicate (heel rise, forward trunk lean, arm drift)?

### 6. Landmine Press
Pressing a barbell anchored at one end in a landmine device. Combines pressing with a curved bar path.
- EMG comparison to standard overhead press: anterior deltoid, serratus anterior, upper/lower trapezius?
- Why is the arced bar path considered more shoulder-friendly than vertical pressing?
- Evidence for use in shoulder impingement or post-surgical shoulder populations?
- Does the landmine press reduce subacromial stress compared to strict overhead press?
- Dosing and programming?

### 7. A-Skip / B-Skip (Running Drills)
High-knee running drills emphasizing hip flexion power (A-skip) and hip flexion with active extension (B-skip).
- EMG activation of hip flexors, gluteals, and hamstrings during skipping drills?
- Evidence for improving running mechanics and sprint performance?
- At what stage of running rehabilitation are these introduced?
- How do these compare to other running drill progressions?

### 8. Single-Leg Hop Tests (Hop for Distance, Triple Hop, Crossover Hop)
Single-leg hopping assessments used as return-to-sport criteria after ACL reconstruction.
- What limb symmetry indices (LSI) are recommended (>90%?) for return to sport?
- Ground reaction forces and muscle activation during single-leg hop?
- Evidence on whether hop test performance predicts re-injury risk?
- Grindem et al. (2016) findings on return-to-sport testing?
- How are these tests used as both assessment and training exercises?
