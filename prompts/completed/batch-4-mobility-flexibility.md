# OpenEvidence Prompt — Mobility & Flexibility Exercises (Batch 4)

I'm studying evidence-based mobility and flexibility exercises across the major body regions. For each exercise below, please provide:

1. **Target tissues** — which muscles, joint capsule, or neural structures are being mobilized/stretched
2. **Evidence for ROM improvement** — what ROM gains are documented in the literature
3. **Optimal dosing** — hold duration, repetitions, frequency from systematic reviews
4. **Mechanism** — does the stretch produce tissue lengthening, neurological tolerance change, or both?
5. **Clinical indications** — which patient populations benefit most
6. **Precautions** — when should this exercise be avoided or modified

Please cite specific studies (author, year, journal) for each exercise.

## Hip & Lower Extremity Mobility

### 1. 90/90 Hip Stretch (Shinbox)
Seated with both hips at 90° flexion and 90° rotation — front leg in external rotation, back leg in internal rotation. Rotate between sides.
- What hip ROM does this position require?
- Does this improve hip IR and ER ROM in clinical studies?
- How does it compare to other hip rotation stretches?
- Is there evidence for use in FAI or hip OA populations?

### 2. Pigeon Stretch (Modified)
Modified pigeon pose: front leg bent across the body, back leg extended behind. Targets external rotators and posterior hip capsule.
- Which muscles are primarily stretched (piriformis, obturator internus, gluteus maximus)?
- What ROM gains are documented?
- Is this safe for patients with hip labral pathology or FAI?
- How does prone pigeon compare to supine figure-4 stretch for the same muscles?

### 3. Couch Stretch (Rectus Femoris / Hip Flexor)
Half-kneeling with rear foot elevated against a wall or couch. Combines hip extension with knee flexion to maximally stretch the rectus femoris.
- Does this produce greater rectus femoris stretch than a standard half-kneeling hip flexor stretch?
- Evidence for improving anterior pelvic tilt or hip extension ROM?
- What populations benefit (anterior knee pain, hip flexor tightness, post-THA)?
- Dosing and safety considerations?

### 4. Ankle Dorsiflexion Mobilization (Banded)
Standing lunge position with resistance band around distal tibia pulling posteriorly. Knee drives forward over toes.
- Does banded joint mobilization improve dorsiflexion more than stretching alone?
- What is the mechanism (posterior talar glide)?
- Evidence from Hoch, Vicenzino, or other ankle mobilization researchers?
- Dosing: sets, reps, hold time from clinical trials?

## Thoracic & Shoulder Mobility

### 5. Thoracic Spine Foam Rolling
Supine on a foam roller positioned at mid-thoracic level, arms crossed or overhead, extending segmentally over the roller.
- Does foam rolling improve thoracic extension ROM? By how much?
- Is the mechanism joint mobilization, muscle relaxation, or both?
- Evidence for use in shoulder impingement or overhead athlete populations?
- Optimal duration and technique (segmental vs continuous rolling)?

### 6. Open Book Stretch (Thoracic Rotation)
Sidelying with hips and knees stacked at 90°, rotating the top arm and thorax open toward the ceiling.
- What thoracic rotation ROM does this achieve?
- EMG or stretch data for internal/external obliques, pectoralis, latissimus?
- Is this effective for improving thoracic rotation in golfers, tennis players, or overhead athletes?
- Dosing from clinical studies?

### 7. Sleeper Stretch (Shoulder Internal Rotation / Posterior Capsule)
Sidelying on the target shoulder, shoulder at 90° forward flexion, passively rotating the forearm toward the floor.
- Evidence for improving glenohumeral internal rotation deficit (GIRD)?
- Does this stretch the posterior capsule, posterior rotator cuff, or both?
- Wilk, Reinold, and Andrews findings on sleeper stretch for overhead athletes?
- Risk of suprascapular nerve compression — when is this contraindicated?

### 8. Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch (Horizontal Adduction)
Standing or seated, pulling the arm across the body to stretch the posterior shoulder.
- Which structures are stretched (posterior deltoid, infraspinatus, posterior capsule)?
- Evidence for improving shoulder IR in overhead athletes?
- How does this compare to the sleeper stretch for GIRD?
- Dosing from shoulder rehabilitation protocols?

### 9. Doorway Pectoralis Stretch
Standing in a doorway with forearm on the frame at 90° shoulder abduction, leaning forward to stretch the pectoralis major.
- Does varying the arm height (60°, 90°, 120°) change which portion of pectoralis is stretched?
- Evidence for improving forward shoulder posture or protracted scapular position?
- EMG or stretch data for clavicular vs sternal pectoralis fibers?
- Dosing and safety (avoid in unstable shoulders)?

## Neural Mobility

### 10. Median Nerve Glide
Sequential movements to mobilize the median nerve: shoulder abduction → elbow extension → wrist/finger extension → cervical lateral flexion away.
- Evidence for use in carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS)?
- Does nerve gliding actually improve nerve excursion or reduce intraneural edema?
- Comparison to carpal tunnel splinting alone or combined with glides?
- Dosing from CTS treatment protocols?
- Precautions: when does nerve gliding reproduce or worsen symptoms?

### 11. Ulnar Nerve Glide
Sequential movements to mobilize the ulnar nerve: shoulder abduction → elbow flexion → wrist extension → cervical lateral flexion away.
- Evidence for cubital tunnel syndrome or ulnar nerve entrapment?
- Mechanism of action: neural sliding vs tensioning?
- Dosing and technique variations from the literature?

### 12. Radial Nerve Glide
Sequential movements to mobilize the radial nerve: shoulder depression → elbow extension → forearm pronation → wrist flexion.
- Evidence for radial tunnel syndrome or lateral epicondylalgia with neural component?
- How do clinicians differentiate radial nerve involvement from pure tendinopathy?
- Dosing from clinical protocols?
