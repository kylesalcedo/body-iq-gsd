# OpenEvidence Prompt — Condition-Specific Exercises (Batch 5)

I'm studying exercises that are prescribed for specific musculoskeletal conditions. For each exercise below, please provide:

1. **Target muscles and EMG activation** (% MVIC where studied)

3. **Mechanism of action** — why this exercise works for this condition

## Rotator Cuff / Shoulder Impingement

### 1. Full Can Exercise (Scaption with Thumbs Up)
Standing shoulder elevation in the scapular plane with thumbs up. Targets supraspinatus.
- EMG comparison of full can vs empty can for supraspinatus activation?
- Escamilla et al. findings on safety and effectiveness?
- Is there a meaningful difference in deltoid vs supraspinatus ratio between the two?
- Evidence for use in supraspinatus tendinopathy or post-repair rehab?
- Dosing from rotator cuff rehabilitation protocols?

### 2. Empty Can Exercise (Scaption with Thumbs Down)
Shoulder elevation in the scapular plane with thumbs down (internal rotation). Classic supraspinatus isolation test.
- Does the empty can position actually isolate supraspinatus more than full can?
- What are the concerns about subacromial impingement in the internally rotated position?
- Current consensus: is this still recommended or has full can replaced it?
- EMG data comparing supraspinatus, deltoid, and upper trapezius activation?

### 3. Prone External Rotation at 90° Abduction
Lying prone with shoulder at 90° abduction off the edge of a table, externally rotating a light weight.
- EMG activation of infraspinatus, teres minor, posterior deltoid, and lower trapezius?
- How does this compare to sidelying ER and standing band ER?
- Is this a late-stage exercise? What prerequisites are needed?
- Evidence for use in rotator cuff tendinopathy, post-repair, or instability?

## Patellofemoral Pain

### 4. Spanish Squat (Belt Squat / Wall-Assisted)
Squat performed with a belt or strap around the knees anchored to a fixed point, allowing posterior lean to reduce patellofemoral load.
- What evidence exists for reduced patellofemoral joint forces compared to standard squats?
- EMG activation of VMO, VL, and VMO:VL ratio?
- Clinical outcomes for patellofemoral pain from RCTs?
- Dosing: depth, resistance, sets/reps from clinical protocols?

### 5. Leg Press (Limited ROM)
Leg press machine restricted to 0-60° knee flexion to minimize patellofemoral stress.
- At what knee flexion angles are patellofemoral forces lowest?
- EMG comparison to squat at matched angles?
- Evidence for use in early-stage patellofemoral pain or post-meniscectomy?
- Foot position effects on VMO vs VL activation?
- Dosing for rehabilitation vs strength contexts?

## Achilles Tendinopathy

### 6. Alfredson Protocol (Eccentric Heel Drops)
Standing on a step edge, performing slow eccentric heel lowering on the affected leg. Both straight-knee (gastrocnemius bias) and bent-knee (soleus bias) versions.
- Original Alfredson et al. (1998) protocol details and outcomes?
- Is the 3×15 reps, twice daily, 12-week protocol still the gold standard?
- How does heavy slow resistance (HSR) from Kongsgaard et al. compare to eccentric-only?
- What is the mechanism — tendon remodeling, neovascularization reduction, pain desensitization?
- When should patients expect improvement and when should they be reassessed?

### 7. Heavy Slow Resistance (HSR) for Achilles Tendinopathy
Progressive heavy resistance training (seated and standing heel raises, leg press calf raises) at 6RM progressing to 3-4 sets of 6-8RM.
- Kongsgaard et al. (2009) comparison to Alfredson eccentric protocol?
- Patient satisfaction and compliance differences?
- Tendon structure changes (ultrasound tissue characterization)?
- Dosing progression over 12 weeks?

## Frozen Shoulder (Adhesive Capsulitis)

### 8. Pendulum Exercises (Codman's)
Leaning forward with the affected arm hanging, creating small circular or pendulum motions using body sway.
- What is the mechanism: joint distraction, synovial fluid circulation, pain gate modulation?
- EMG data: are shoulder muscles truly relaxed during pendulum exercises?
- Evidence for effectiveness in early-stage frozen shoulder?
- Dosing: frequency, duration, amplitude from clinical protocols?
- Are pendulum exercises more effective with or without a handheld weight?

### 9. Wall Walks (Wall Climb / Finger Walking)
Facing or perpendicular to a wall, walking the fingers up the wall to progressively increase shoulder flexion or abduction ROM.
- Does wall walking improve ROM faster than passive stretching alone?
- Evidence for use in frozen shoulder, post-surgical stiffness?
- At what point in frozen shoulder stages is active ROM exercise introduced?
- Dosing: repetitions, frequency, how far to push into discomfort?

## Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

- Does nerve gliding reduce intraneural pressure or improve nerve excursion?
- Dosing from Akalin (2002), Baysal (2006), or Horng (2011) studies?
- Can gliding exercises delay or prevent surgical intervention?

### 11. Wrist Neutral Splinting + Exercise Protocol
Combination of nighttime neutral wrist splinting with daytime tendon/nerve gliding exercises.
- Evidence for combined splinting + exercise vs either alone?
- Optimal splint position (neutral vs 20° extension)?
- Duration of splint use recommended?
- When should conservative management be abandoned for surgical referral?
