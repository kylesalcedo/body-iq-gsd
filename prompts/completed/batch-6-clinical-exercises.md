# OpenEvidence Prompt — Clinical Exercise Justification (Batch 6)

I'm expanding a clinical rehabilitation exercise database. For each exercise below, please provide:

1. **Target muscles and EMG activation levels** (% MVIC where studied)
2. **Evidence-based dosing** — sets, reps, frequency, hold times from RCTs or systematic reviews
3. **Key coaching cues** supported by biomechanical research
4. **Regressions** (easier variations) and **progressions** (harder variations)
5. **Clinical indications** — which patient populations benefit most
6. **Contraindications or precautions**
7. **Key differentiator** — what makes this exercise uniquely valuable compared to alternatives

Please cite specific studies (author, year, journal) for each exercise.

## Spinal Mobility & Motor Control

### 1. Cat-Cow (Quadruped Spinal Flexion-Extension)
Alternating between full spinal flexion (cat) and extension (cow) from a quadruped position. Used for spinal segmental mobility, motor control awareness, and pain modulation.
- What is the evidence for spinal mobility improvement in LBP populations?
- EMG activation of erector spinae, multifidus, rectus abdominis during each phase?
- How does this compare to other spinal mobility exercises for pain outcomes?
- Is there evidence supporting its use as a warm-up or movement preparation?
- Dosing from clinical studies?

### 2. Quadruped Rockback (Child's Pose from Quadruped)
From quadruped, sit hips back toward heels while maintaining arm position. Targets lumbar flexion mobility, hip flexion ROM, and latissimus/thoracolumbar fascia lengthening.
- What hip flexion ROM is achieved in end-range rockback?
- Evidence for use in lumbar stenosis or flexion-biased LBP programs?
- EMG of spinal extensors — is this a relative rest position for multifidus/ES?
- How does this compare to child's pose (yoga) biomechanically?

### 3. Thoracic Spine Rotation in Quadruped (Thread the Needle)
From quadruped, rotate the thoracic spine by threading one arm under the body, then opening up toward the ceiling. Targets thoracic rotation mobility.
- What thoracic rotation ROM is achieved vs. seated or supine rotation?
- Does the quadruped position effectively lock out lumbar rotation?
- EMG of thoracic rotators (rotatores, multifidus, internal/external obliques)?
- Evidence for improving thoracic rotation in overhead athletes, neck pain, or shoulder impingement populations?
- How does this compare to open book stretch for thoracic rotation?

### 4. Thoracic Extension with Hip Hinge on Wall
Standing facing a wall, hinge at the hips to place forearms on the wall at shoulder height, then press the chest through toward the wall to achieve upper thoracic extension. Also described as a "wall prayer stretch."
- Does this position effectively isolate upper thoracic extension vs. lumbar extension?
- EMG activation of thoracic extensors, serratus anterior, lower trapezius?
- Evidence for use in thoracic hyperkyphosis correction?
- How does this compare to foam roller thoracic extension?
- Dosing recommendations from postural correction studies?

## Cervical Spine

### 5. Supine Chin Tuck with Liftoff (Cervical Flexor Endurance)
Supine chin tuck with head lifted 1-2 inches off the surface, maintaining the tucked position. Tests and trains deep cervical flexor endurance.
- What is the relationship to the deep neck flexor endurance test (Garrett et al.)?
- EMG comparison of DCF activation in supine liftoff vs. standard chin tuck without liftoff?
- Normative hold times and how they correlate with neck pain severity?
- Is this a valid progression from pressure biofeedback CCF training?
- Dosing: hold duration, repetitions, and progression criteria?

## Posterior Chain & Hip

### 6. Foam Roller Bridges
Supine bridge with feet elevated on a foam roller. The unstable surface increases posterior chain coordination demands and preferentially activates gluteus maximus over hamstrings.
- EMG comparison of gluteus maximus, hamstrings, and erector spinae vs. standard bridges?
- Does the unstable surface actually shift activation ratios toward glutes?
- Evidence for use in gluteal activation programs for LBP or hip pathology?
- How does this compare to bridges on a Swiss ball?
- Dosing and progression criteria?

### 7. Kettlebell Good Mornings
Standing with a kettlebell held at the chest (goblet position) or behind the neck, perform a hip hinge to ~90° of hip flexion while maintaining neutral spine.
- EMG activation of erector spinae, gluteus maximus, hamstrings compared to barbell good mornings or RDL?
- What spinal loading is produced relative to deadlifts?
- Evidence for use in posterior chain development for LBP prevention?
- Is the kettlebell position safer than barbell for rehabilitation populations?
- Dosing for rehabilitation vs. strength training contexts?

### 8. Single Leg Hip Hinge with Bench (Single Leg RDL with Support)
Single leg hip hinge with the rear foot elevated on a bench for balance support. Targets unilateral posterior chain strength while reducing balance demands.
- How does this compare to unsupported single-leg RDL for gluteus maximus and hamstring activation?
- Does the bench support allow higher loading without balance limitation?
- Evidence for use in hamstring injury prevention or return-to-sport programs?
- EMG comparison to split squat and Bulgarian split squat?

### 9. Hip Abduction/Extension with Sliders
Standing or quadruped exercises using friction-reducing sliders to perform hip abduction and/or extension through a sliding arc. Emphasizes eccentric control through the return phase.
- EMG of gluteus medius, gluteus maximus, TFL during slider abduction vs. banded exercises?
- Does the sliding component change the eccentric loading profile?
- Evidence for use in hip tendinopathy or ITB syndrome?
- How does this compare to standing cable hip abduction or side-lying hip abduction?
- Dosing recommendations?

## Lower Extremity

### 10. Single Leg Squat / Pistol Squat (Eccentric Emphasis)
Unilateral squat progressing from box-assisted to full pistol squat. Eccentric emphasis — controlled lowering over 3-5 seconds.
- EMG of quadriceps (VMO vs VL), gluteus medius, hip adductors during single-leg squat?
- What knee valgus demands differentiate this from bilateral squat?
- Evidence for use in patellofemoral pain, ACL rehabilitation, or return-to-sport?
- How do eccentric single-leg squats compare to Spanish squats for patellar tendinopathy?
- Dosing for eccentric emphasis protocol?

### 11. Knee Split Squat Isometric Holds (Ligament Strengthening)
Deep split squat position with isometric holds of 45+ seconds. Targets knee ligament conditioning through sustained tensile loading. Progressive dosing up to 5 × 45s.
- What is the evidence for isometric loading improving ligament mechanical properties?
- Which knee ligaments (ACL, PCL, MCL, LCL) are loaded in the split squat position?
- Is there a dose-response relationship for hold duration and ligament adaptation?
- Evidence from Keith Baar or similar collagen synthesis research applied to knee ligaments?
- How does this compare to heavy slow resistance for tendon/ligament adaptation?

### 12. Heel-Toe Walking (Tandem Gait)
Walking in a straight line placing the heel of one foot directly in front of the toes of the other foot. Tests and trains dynamic balance and proprioception.
- What balance systems (vestibular, proprioceptive, visual) are challenged by tandem gait?
- Evidence for use in fall prevention in elderly populations?
- Normative performance values and correlation with fall risk?
- How does this compare to single-leg stance for balance training effectiveness?
- Dosing: distance, repetitions, progression criteria?

### 13. Backwards Walking (Retro Walking)
Walking in reverse, emphasizing quadriceps eccentric control and knee extension loading. Used in knee rehabilitation and patellofemoral pain.
- EMG of quadriceps, particularly VMO, during backwards walking vs. forward walking?
- Evidence for use in patellofemoral pain, post-TKA, or knee OA populations?
- What is the incline backwards walking protocol (treadmill)?
- Biomechanical differences — reduced patellofemoral joint reaction force?
- Dosing: speed, duration, incline progression?

### 14. Frog Pose Holds (Hip Adductor Stretch)
Wide-knee position on the floor (prone or quadruped) with hips externally rotated and abducted, holding for sustained stretch of the hip adductors (adductor longus, brevis, gracilis, pectineus).
- What hip adductor ROM is achieved in full frog pose?
- Evidence for use in groin injury prevention or hip impingement?
- How does this compare to standing adductor stretch or butterfly stretch?
- Is there evidence for sustained holds (>60s) vs. dynamic stretching for adductor flexibility?
- Dosing: hold duration, frequency, contraindications (hip labral pathology)?
