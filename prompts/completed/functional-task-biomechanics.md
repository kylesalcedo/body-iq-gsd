# OpenEvidence Prompt — Biomechanics of Functional Tasks (ADLs/IADLs)

I'm studying the biomechanical demands of common functional tasks to better understand the movement and muscle requirements for daily activities. For each task below, please provide evidence-based information on:

- **Required joint movements** and approximate ROM thresholds from clinical literature
- **Primary muscles activated** and their approximate activation levels where studied
- **Strength thresholds** for independent performance where data exists
- **Common compensatory patterns** when strength or ROM is insufficient
- **Clinical significance** — which patient populations commonly lose this ability, and what predicts recovery
- **Standardized assessment tools** that measure this task (e.g., Timed Up and Go, Berg Balance Scale items, Functional Independence Measure)

---

## Task 1: Carrying and Lifting Objects
(e.g., carrying groceries, lifting a laundry basket from floor to counter)

- What joint movements and muscles are required for bilateral carrying at waist height vs one-sided carrying?
- What does the biomechanics literature say about grip strength requirements for carrying tasks?
- What trunk stabilization demands exist during loaded carrying (farmer's carry pattern)?
- How does load position (front carry vs side carry vs overhead) change the muscle activation pattern?
- What are the minimum strength thresholds for safe lifting from floor to waist (NIOSH guidelines or equivalent)?

## Task 2: Dressing — Upper Body
(putting on/removing a shirt, buttoning, zipping)

- What shoulder ROM is required for overhead shirt donning (specific flexion, abduction, IR, ER ranges)?
- What hand and finger dexterity demands exist for buttoning and zipping?
- Which muscles are critical for the "hand behind back" pattern needed for bra fastening and tucking in shirts?
- What are common post-surgical ROM deficits that prevent independent dressing (post-shoulder arthroplasty, post-mastectomy)?
- Evidence on required pinch strength for button manipulation?

## Task 3: Dressing — Lower Body
(putting on pants, socks, shoes, tying laces)

- What hip flexion and knee flexion ROM is required to reach the feet?
- What trunk flexion and balance demands exist for standing sock and shoe donning?
- Which muscles are critical: hip flexors for bringing foot to hand, core for balance, hand intrinsics for laces?
- What compensatory strategies do patients commonly use post-THA or with hip OA?
- Minimum sit-and-reach or hip flexion thresholds for independent lower body dressing?

## Task 4: Bathing and Hygiene
(showering, washing hair, reaching behind back, toileting transfers)

- What shoulder ROM is required for washing hair (overhead reaching) and reaching behind the back (IR + extension)?
- What are the balance demands during standing in a shower (single-leg stance moments while washing feet)?
- Required grip strength for washcloth wringing and faucet turning?
- What sit-to-stand strength is needed for toilet transfers (lower seat height than standard chair)?
- Evidence on which personal hygiene ADL components are lost first in aging populations?

## Task 5: Floor Transfers
(sit-to-floor-to-sit, getting up after a fall)

- What joint ROM is required: hip flexion, knee flexion, ankle dorsiflexion minimums?
- What are the muscle strength demands for floor-to-stand transitions (quadriceps, gluteals, upper extremity push)?
- What does the literature say about floor transfer ability as a predictor of mortality and falls risk (de Brito et al. sitting-rising test)?
- Which strategy patterns exist (half-kneel, hands-and-knees, deep squat) and what are their biomechanical demands?
- Minimum strength thresholds for independent floor transfers in older adults?

## Task 6: Running and Jogging

- What is the ankle-knee-hip kinetic chain activation pattern during running gait?
- Peak muscle forces during stance phase: gastrocnemius, soleus, quadriceps, gluteals?
- Required ankle dorsiflexion ROM for running compared to walking?
- Hip extension and hip abductor demands — what does EMG show for gluteus medius during running?
- Common muscle weakness patterns that lead to running injuries (weak hip abductors → knee valgus, weak calf complex → Achilles tendinopathy)?

## Task 7: Getting In and Out of Bed
(rolling, transitioning from supine to sitting, standing from bed height)

- What is the typical movement sequence: supine → sidelying → sitting → standing?
- Which muscles drive each phase (trunk rotators for rolling, lateral trunk flexors for coming to sit, hip and knee extensors for standing)?
- What are the balance and strength demands at each transition point?
- Evidence on bed mobility as an early indicator of functional decline?
- Required trunk rotation ROM and upper extremity push strength?

## Task 8: Sustained Overhead Work
(painting a ceiling, putting items on a high shelf, hanging curtains)

- What shoulder elevation ROM and muscular endurance is required for sustained overhead tasks?
- What does EMG show for rotator cuff and scapular muscle fatigue during prolonged overhead work?
- What cervical spine demands exist from sustained neck extension?
- Evidence on shoulder impingement risk during overhead work from occupational health literature?
- What core and lower extremity stabilization demands exist for standing overhead work?
