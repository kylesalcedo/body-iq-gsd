# OpenEvidence Prompt — Functional Task Biomechanics

Our movement knowledge system models functional tasks (ADLs/IADLs) and maps them to the movements and muscles required for each task. We currently have 11 tasks:

1. Reaching overhead
2. Pushing up from chair
3. Gripping a cup
4. Opening a jar
5. Typing
6. Walking
7. Stair climbing
8. Squat to stand
9. Breathing
10. Single-leg balance
11. Posture maintenance

We need to add the following functional tasks. For each one, please provide evidence-based information on:

- **Required joint movements** (list each joint motion involved, classified as "essential" or "supportive")
- **Primary muscles activated** and their approximate activation levels
- **Required ROM at each joint** (minimum thresholds from clinical literature)
- **Strength thresholds** where studied (e.g., minimum quadriceps strength for independent stair climbing)
- **Common compensatory patterns** when strength or ROM is insufficient
- **Clinical significance** — which patient populations commonly lose this ability, and what predicts recovery

---

## Task 1: Carrying / Lifting Objects (e.g., carrying groceries, lifting a laundry basket)

- What joint movements and muscles are required for bilateral carrying at waist height vs. one-sided carrying?
- What does the biomechanics literature say about grip strength requirements for carrying tasks?
- What trunk stabilization demands exist during loaded carrying (farmer's carry pattern)?
- How does load position (front carry vs side carry vs overhead) change the muscle activation pattern?
- What are the minimum strength thresholds for safe lifting from floor to waist (NIOSH guidelines or equivalent)?

## Task 2: Dressing — Upper Body (putting on/removing a shirt, buttoning, zipping)

- What shoulder ROM is required for overhead shirt donning? (specific flexion/abduction/IR/ER ranges)
- What hand/finger dexterity demands exist for buttoning and zipping?
- Which muscles are critical for the "hand behind back" pattern needed for bra fastening, tucking in shirts?
- What are common post-surgical ROM deficits that prevent independent dressing (post-shoulder arthroplasty, post-mastectomy)?
- Evidence on required pinch strength for button manipulation?

## Task 3: Dressing — Lower Body (putting on pants, socks, shoes, tying laces)

- What hip flexion and knee flexion ROM is required to reach the feet?
- What trunk flexion and balance demands exist for standing sock/shoe donning?
- Which muscles are critical: hip flexors for foot-to-hand, core for balance, hand intrinsics for laces?
- What compensatory strategies do patients use post-THA or with hip OA?
- Minimum sit-and-reach or hip flexion thresholds for independent lower body dressing?

## Task 4: Bathing / Hygiene (showering, washing hair, reaching behind back, toileting)

- What shoulder ROM is required for washing hair (overhead) and reaching behind back (IR + extension)?
- What are the balance demands during standing shower (single-leg stance moments)?
- Required grip strength for washcloth wringing, faucet turning?
- What sit-to-stand strength is needed for toilet transfers (lower seat height than standard chair)?
- Evidence on which ADL components are lost first in aging populations?

## Task 5: Floor Transfers (sit-to-floor-to-sit, getting up from the floor)

- What joint ROM is required: hip flexion, knee flexion, ankle dorsiflexion?
- What are the muscle strength demands for floor-to-stand transitions (quadriceps, gluteals, upper extremity push)?
- What does the literature say about floor transfer ability as a predictor of mortality/falls risk (de Brito et al. sitting-rising test)?
- Which strategy patterns exist (half-kneel, hands-and-knees, deep squat) and what are their biomechanical demands?
- Minimum strength thresholds for independent floor transfers in older adults?

## Task 6: Running / Jogging

- What is the ankle-knee-hip kinetic chain activation pattern during running gait?
- Peak muscle forces: gastrocnemius, soleus, quadriceps, gluteals during stance phase?
- Required ankle dorsiflexion ROM for running vs walking?
- Hip extension and hip abductor demands — what does EMG show for gluteus medius during running?
- Common muscle weakness patterns that lead to running injuries (weak hip abductors → knee valgus, weak calf → Achilles tendinopathy)?

## Task 7: Getting In/Out of Bed (rolling, transitioning to sitting, standing from bed height)

- What movement sequence is used: supine → sidelying → sitting → standing?
- Which muscles drive each phase (trunk rotators for rolling, lateral trunk flexors for sit-up, hip/knee extensors for stand)?
- What are the balance and strength demands at each transition point?
- Evidence on bed mobility as an early indicator of functional decline?
- Required trunk rotation ROM and upper extremity push strength?

## Task 8: Overhead Work (painting a ceiling, putting items on a high shelf, hanging curtains)

- What shoulder elevation ROM and endurance is required for sustained overhead tasks?
- What does EMG show for rotator cuff and scapular muscle fatigue during prolonged overhead work?
- What cervical spine demands exist (sustained extension)?
- Evidence on shoulder impingement risk during overhead work from occupational health literature?
- What core stabilization demands exist for standing overhead work?

- **ICF classification** (if a standardized activity/participation code exists)
