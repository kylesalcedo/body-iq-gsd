# OpenEvidence Prompt — Hand Intrinsic Muscle Movement Roles

I'm studying the specific muscle contributions for fine hand movements to improve my understanding of hand rehabilitation. For each movement below, which muscles act as primary movers, secondary movers, and stabilizers?

## 1. Finger Adduction (MCP joint)
- Which palmar interossei specifically adduct which fingers (1st, 2nd, 3rd)?
- Does the adductor pollicis contribute to index finger adduction, or only thumb adduction?
- What stabilizer muscles are active during finger adduction?

## 2. Thumb Adduction
- Is adductor pollicis the sole primary mover?
- What are the roles of the oblique vs transverse heads?
- Does the first palmar interosseous contribute?
- What % MVIC or force data exists for thumb adduction tasks?

## 3. Finger DIP Flexion
- Is flexor digitorum profundus the sole muscle crossing the DIP joint?
- What is the FDP activation level during isolated DIP flexion vs power grip?
- Do the lumbricals or interossei play any stabilizing role at the DIP?

## 4. Finger DIP Extension  
- Is extension at the DIP achieved via the lateral bands (from lumbricals and interossei)?
- Can extensor digitorum alone fully extend the DIP, or is intrinsic muscle contribution required?
- What happens to DIP extension when intrinsics are paralyzed (as in ulnar nerve palsy)?

## 5. Power Grip vs Precision Grip Muscle Activation Patterns
- How do the activation patterns differ between power grip (full fist) and precision grip (pinch)?
- What is the relative contribution of extrinsic flexors (FDS, FDP) vs intrinsics (lumbricals, interossei) in each grip type?
- What EMG data exists comparing thenar muscle activation in tip pinch vs lateral pinch vs three-jaw chuck?

## 6. Scapular Downward Rotation
- Beyond rhomboids and levator scapulae, does pectoralis minor contribute to downward rotation?
- What is the relative force contribution of rhomboid major vs minor?
- When is downward rotation functionally important (reaching behind back, arm at side)?

## 7. Upper Cervical Extension
- What muscles specifically extend the atlantooccipital and atlantoaxial joints?
- Do the suboccipital muscles (rectus capitis posterior major/minor, obliquus capitis superior) all contribute equally?
- What is semispinalis capitis activation level during upper cervical extension vs full cervical extension?

Please cite specific studies with author, year, and journal where available.
