# OpenEvidence Prompt — Scapular Stabilization: Deep Expansion

## Context

Body IQ's workout planner grid has ALL six scapulothoracic movements represented (elevation, depression, protraction, retraction, upward rotation, downward rotation) — but each has only 1–2 exercises. Scapular stabilization is a high-value clinical area (shoulder impingement, rotator cuff, throwing athletes, postural rehab), and the grid should show 4–8 evidence-based options per scapular movement.

Please provide evidence-based exercises for each scapular movement. For each exercise, provide:

1. **Exercise name** and a 1–2 sentence description
2. **Primary scapular mover** (trapezius fiber direction, rhomboids, serratus anterior, levator scapulae, pec minor, lat) with EMG % MVIC where known
3. **Secondary muscles** and stabilizers
4. **Equipment**
5. **Evidence-based dosing** (sets, reps, frequency) from RCTs or systematic reviews
6. **Coaching cues** (2–4 concise verbal/tactile cues)
7. **Regressions and progressions**
8. **Clinical indications** — shoulder impingement, rotator cuff repair post-op, scapular dyskinesis, thoracic outlet, etc.
9. **Citations** — prioritize Reinold, Kibler, Ludewig, Cools, Ellenbecker, Escamilla

## Scapular movements to expand

### Scapular Upward Rotation (need 6+ exercises)

Primary drivers: upper trap, lower trap, serratus anterior (the "force couple").
- What exercises produce the highest serratus anterior : upper trap EMG ratio? (Key for impingement rehab)
- Evidence for wall slides, serratus push-ups, scap push-ups, and their variations
- Dynamic hug, punch-outs, overhead reaches — which are best-supported?
- Progressions from wall → incline → prone → overhead
- Kibler's serratus progression literature

### Scapular Downward Rotation (need 5+ exercises)

Primary drivers: rhomboids, lat, lower trap (eccentrically), pec minor.
- Often neglected in rehab — what's the clinical case for training it?
- Band-resisted downward rotation, lat pulldowns with proper scapular positioning, archer pulls
- Which exercises are validated for the overhead athlete (rotator cuff tendinopathy)?

### Scapular Elevation (need 5+ exercises)

Primary drivers: upper trap, levator scapulae.
- Loaded shrugs: barbell vs dumbbell vs trap bar — EMG and biomechanical differences
- Farmer's carries and overhead carries as functional elevation training
- Should upper trap be trained directly in everyone, or selectively? (Janda vs. modern views)
- Evidence from Andersen et al., Schoenfeld et al., Lehman et al.

### Scapular Depression (need 5+ exercises)

Primary drivers: lower trap, lat, pec minor (eccentric).
- Scap pull-ups, dead hangs with active depression, straight-arm pulldowns
- Evidence for prone Y raises vs. prone I raises for lower trap activation
- How to isolate lower trap when upper trap is dominant (Cools' inhibition techniques)

### Scapular Protraction (need 5+ exercises)

Primary driver: serratus anterior.
- Serratus wall slides, push-up plus, scapular push-ups, supine punches
- EMG comparisons from Ludewig/Reinold/Decker literature
- Why serratus anterior is the "forgotten muscle" and how to target it without pec dominance

### Scapular Retraction (need 5+ exercises)

Primary drivers: rhomboids, middle trap, lower trap.
- Prone T-raises vs prone row vs inverted row vs band pull-apart — EMG comparisons
- Face pulls with scapular retraction emphasis
- Role of retraction in thoracic outlet and cervicogenic headache protocols

## Bonus: scapular force couples

Please also provide exercises that train **scapular force couples** (multiple scapular muscles acting together) rather than isolation:

- Upper trap + lower trap + serratus anterior (upward rotation force couple)
- Rhomboids + lower trap (downward rotation + retraction)
- Serratus anterior + lower trap (scapular stability during overhead motion)

Cite the Kibler "Scapular Dyskinesis" series and Cools' scapular muscle balance literature.

## Deliverable format

Markdown, H3 per scapular movement, H4 per exercise. Include a summary table at the end with:

- Movement
- Exercise name
- Primary muscle (% MVIC if known)
- Evidence level
- Key citation

Flag any movement where 4+ evidence-based exercises aren't available — we'd rather know the gap is real than receive speculative content.
