Retrieved: 2026-03-29

## ROTATOR CUFF / SHOULDER IMPINGEMENT

### 1. Full Can Exercise (Scaption with Thumbs Up)

**EMG Activation:**
- Supraspinatus: similar activation between full can and empty can (no significant difference) [1]
- Middle deltoid: 52±27% MVIC (full can) vs 77±44% MVIC (empty can, p=0.001) [1]
- Supraspinatus-to-deltoid ratio superior with full can [2-3]

**Dosing:**
- Holmgren et al. (2012): eccentric RC exercises 3×15 reps, 1-2x daily, 12 weeks [4]
- GRASP trial (2021): progressive resistance 5 days/week, 2 recovery days, 12-16 weeks [5]
- Scoping review (2024): typical 2-7x/week, 1-3 sets of 4-30 reps, 4-16 weeks [6]

**Mechanism:**
- Full can (ER) reduces subacromial impingement by promoting scapular upward rotation and posterior tilt [7]
- Empty can produces significantly more scapular anterior tipping and internal rotation, decreasing subacromial space [7]
- Scapular retraction during full can enhances supraspinatus force production [3]

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- Holmgren: Constant-Murley improved 24 vs 9 points (Δ15, 95% CI 8.5-20.6, p<0.001) [4]
- 69% successful outcomes vs 24% controls (OR 7.6, p<0.001) [4]
- Only 20% proceeded to surgery vs 63% controls (OR 7.7, p<0.001) [4]

**Coaching Cues:**
- Elevate arm in scapular plane (30-45° anterior to coronal plane)
- Maintain thumb-up position (external rotation)
- Keep scapula retracted, avoid shoulder shrugging
- Slow controlled movement (3s concentric, 3s eccentric)
- Stop at 90° or pain-free range

### 2. Empty Can Exercise (Scaption with Thumbs Down)

**EMG:**
- Does NOT selectively isolate supraspinatus — 9 other shoulder muscles equally activated [8]
- Smallest supraspinatus-to-deltoid ratio (0.8) compared to other positions [2]
- Middle deltoid: 77±44% MVIC; posterior deltoid: 54±24% MVIC [1]

**Concerns:**
- Internally rotated position narrows subacromial space [7]
- Greater scapular internal rotation and anterior tilt [3]

**Consensus:**
- Full can has largely replaced empty can for rehabilitation [1]
- Empty can may still be useful for recruiting middle deltoid when that is the goal [1]

### 3. Prone External Rotation at 90° Abduction

**EMG Activation:**
- Prone horizontal ABD at 100° with full ER: supraspinatus 82% MVIC, middle deltoid 87% MVIC, posterior deltoid 88% MVIC [9]
- Sidelying ER: infraspinatus 62% MVIC, teres minor 67% MVIC [9]
- Sidelying wiper: infraspinatus 55.98% while minimizing MT and posterior deltoid [10]
- Teres minor more important as ER at 90° ABD (TM:infraspinatus ratio 1.21±0.23) vs 0° ABD [11]

**Comparison:**
- Prone ER: greater muscle activity than seated ER for infraspinatus and posterior deltoid [12]
- At 90° ABD: upper trapezius and serratus anterior significantly increase [13]

**Prerequisites:**
- Late-stage exercise requiring: adequate scapular stability, pain-free 90° ABD, posterior capsule flexibility [14]

## PATELLOFEMORAL PAIN

### 4. Spanish Squat (Belt Squat)

**Evidence:**
- Direct "Spanish squat" evidence lacking in literature
- PF forces lowest at lower knee flexion angles (0.6× BW during walking) [15]
- Reducing knee flexion during squats decreases PF forces by 14.4% (p<0.05) [16]
- VMO:VL ratios approaching 1:1 in CKC exercises [17-18]

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- APTA 2020 guidelines: hip and quad exercises primary treatment [19]
- High-volume protocols (3×30+ reps, 3x/week) most effective [19]
- Exercise reduces activity-related pain by MD -1.46 (95% CI -2.39 to -0.54) [20-21]

**Dosing:**
- 0-60° knee flexion range, 3×15-30 reps, 3-5 days/week, 6-12 weeks [19][22]

### 5. Leg Press (Limited ROM)

**Optimal Angles:**
- PF forces lowest 0-60° knee flexion
- Maximum VMO:VL ratio at 60° knee flexion in CKC [23]
- 60-90° creates greatest PF stress [24]

**EMG:**
- CKC: VMO:VL ratios approached 1:1, significantly better than OKC [17]
- PFP patients: greater VMO and VL activity during WB vs healthy (compensatory) [25]
- Hip adduction during squats: VMO:VL ratio 1.14:1 [18], increases VMO without increasing VL [26]

**Dosing:**
- 0-60° knee flexion, 3×15-30 reps, 3-5 days/week, progress depth as tolerated [19][22]

## ACHILLES TENDINOPATHY

### 6. Alfredson Protocol (Eccentric Heel Drops)

**Protocol:**
- 3×15 reps, 2x daily (180 total reps/day), 7 days/week, 12 weeks [27-28]
- Both straight-knee (gastroc) and bent-knee (soleus) versions
- Slow speed, progressive load when pain-free [28]
- "Do-as-tolerated" protocol (avg 112 reps/day) produced similar outcomes [27]

**Outcomes:**
- VISA-A: 49.2 → 83.6 at 5 years (p<0.001) [29]
- 39.7% completely pain-free at 5 years [29]
- Significant improvement baseline to 1 year (49.2→75.0), further 1→5 years (75.0→83.4) [29]

**Mechanism:**
- Tendon remodeling and collagen reorganization [29-30]
- Reduction in neovascularization [29-30]
- Pain desensitization through loading [30]
- Improved neuromuscular performance — only mechanism consistently associated with improvement [30]

**Timeline:**
- Gradual improvement over 12 weeks, continued gains to 52 weeks [29][31]
- Reassess if no improvement by 6-12 weeks [32]
- Strong evidence but heterogeneity in protocols [28]

### 7. Heavy Slow Resistance (HSR)

**Protocol (Beyer et al.):**
- 3 bilateral full-range heel-raise exercises at 6s per rep [31-32]
- Seated calf raise (bent knee), standing barbell (straight knee), leg press calf raise (straight knee)
- Week 1: 3×15 → Weeks 2-3: 3×12 → Weeks 4-5: 4×10 → Weeks 6-8: 4×8 → Weeks 9-12: 4×6

**Comparison to Alfredson:**
- VISA-A improved 27.0 (ECC) vs 34.0 (HSR) at 52 weeks (NS) [31-32]
- VAS pain during running: -38 (ECC) vs -49 (HSR) [32]
- Both reduced tendon thickness and neovascularization [31]

**Compliance:**
- HSR: 92% vs ECC: 78% (p<0.005) [31]
- Patient satisfaction 12 weeks: HSR 100% vs ECC 0% (p=0.052) [31]
- 52 weeks: HSR 98% vs ECC 76% (p=0.10) [32]

**Tendon Changes:**
- HSR: reduced Doppler area/AP diameter, greater collagen turnover evidence [30]
- HSR may be more likely to produce tendon adaptation [30]
- Wilson et al. meta-analysis: moderate evidence of no significant difference [33]

## FROZEN SHOULDER

### 8. Pendulum Exercises (Codman's)
- Limited high-quality evidence retrieved
- Proposed mechanisms: joint distraction, synovial fluid circulation, pain gate modulation
- Typically prescribed in early freezing phase
- Dosing: 5-10 minutes, 2-3x daily, ± 1-2 lb weight

### 9. Wall Walks (Wall Climb)
- Limited RCT evidence retrieved
- Late freezing or early thawing phase
- Dosing: 3×10 reps, 1-2x daily, progress height as tolerated
- Work into mild discomfort but not sharp pain

## CARPAL TUNNEL SYNDROME

### 10. Nerve and Tendon Gliding Exercises
- Limited systematic review evidence retrieved
- Historical studies (Akalin 2002, Baysal 2006, Horng 2011): modest improvements with splinting
- Mechanism: reduce intraneural pressure, improve nerve excursion, prevent adhesions
- Dosing: 5-10 reps each position, 3-5x daily, 6-12 weeks + nighttime splinting

### 11. Wrist Neutral Splinting + Exercise Protocol
- Neutral splinting (0-20° extension) nightly for 6-12 weeks + daytime gliding
- Surgical referral: severe symptoms >6 months, thenar atrophy, persistent numbness, failed 3-6 months conservative, severe electrodiagnostic findings
